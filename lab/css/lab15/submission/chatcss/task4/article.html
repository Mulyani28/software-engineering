Taylor Swift's "Fearless (Taylor's Version)" debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Chart

In an impressive feat for the music industry, Taylor Swift's re-recorded version of her album "Fearless" has debuted at 1 on the Billboard 200 chart. The album, titled "Fearless (Taylor's Version)", marks Swift's ninth No. 1 album and sets a new record for the fastest accumulation of three No. 1 albums by a woman. The album, which features 26 tracks including six previously unreleased songs, was released on April 9th, 2021, through Republic Records. The album is a re-recording of Swift's 2008 album "Fearless" which was originally released through Big Machine Records, and includes all of the original tracks plus new recordings of the album's hit singles such as "Love Story" and "You Belong with Me".


The decision to re-record "Fearless" came after Swift's master recordings were sold by her former label, Big Machine Records, to music executive Scooter Braun's Ithaca Holdings in 2019. This move caused a public feud between Swift and Braun, who Swift accused of bullying her throughout her career. In a statement about the re-recording, Swift said, "I'm thrilled to tell you that my new version of 'Fearless' is done and will be with you soon. It has 26 songs including 6 never before released songs from the vault. 'Love Story (Taylor's Version)' will be out tonight." Swift has since released two other tracks from the album, "Mr. Perfectly Fine" and "Wildest Dreams (Taylor's Version)". The success of "Fearless (Taylor's Version)" is not only a testament to Swift's loyal fan base, but also to the power of re-recording as a means for artists to regain control of their music. Swift's move to re-record her albums has been seen as a response to the ongoing issue of artists losing control of their master recordings, which are often sold to labels as part of recording contracts.


Swift has been an outspoken advocate for artists' rights and has used her platform to speak out against the exploitative practices of the music industry. In November 2020, Swift was named Artist of the Year at the American Music Awards, where she spoke out against the sale of her masters and the need for greater artist control.
